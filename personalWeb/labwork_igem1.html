Project I -- An Indefinite Genetic Counter (Independent Design) – in the testing and simulation stage

Abstract: This indefinite counter uses recombinases, such as Cre and flpe, which can invert DNA between two oppositely oriented cognate recognition sites, such as loxP and FRT sites, respectively. In my counter design, recombinase gene (rec) Cre is downstream and the rec Flpe is upstream of a promoter (PBAD), and they are fused to an ssrA tag respectively that causes rapid protein degradation, and followed by a transcriptional terminator (Term) with gfp and rfp to show the consequence of the process. The promoter PBAD is placed between forward and reverse recombinase recognition sites (lox Pf and lox Pr), and then, this DNA sequence is placed between forward and reverse recombinase recognition sites (FRTf and FRTr), forming a definite counting unit. Upon expression of the rec Cre by an upstream promoter, the entire counter is inverted between the inner recognition sites. Then, gfp was expressed in a limited amount. When the promoter is inverted, further expression of rec Cre protein and gfp ceases, and DNA orientation is fixed. In the second run, such steps are repeated, but at this time recombinase flpe and rfp will express instead of the cre and gfp. When the flpe expresses, the promoter is inverted again, showed in the figure as below.

                     

In addition, I think one must be pointed out is that some unrelated sequences must be added between two inner recombinase recognition sites in order to reduce mutual impact of the recombinase recognition.

In the design, I placed them mentioned above on pBAC plasmids that are maintained as single copy episomes. These circuits utilize PBAD, so that pulses of arabinose constitute inputs to the circuit

Differences and creative points:

Admittedly, it looks like the design in the DIC 3-counter, especially the use of the similar basic components. However, there are many differences between them.

First, the aim is different. In this circuit, the counter could count indefinitely. It is like a genetic oscillation. However, as I know, many oscillations are based on the principle of reverse feedback while it seems that few would use recombinases to realize such functions. I think it may provide new orientation.

Secondly, the design is simple and feasible theoretically. By making use of one promoter, two recombinase sequences and ssrA tags, it may count more numbers before leakage phenomenon becomes notable. If controlled appropriately, ssrA tags will accelerate the degradation of the recombinases in order to make the counter invert continually.

Moreover, it provides sequential control for the more complex biological process. Just as the clock in the Single Chip Microcomputer, this design, if modified accurately, would provide such ‘clock’ in the future biocomputer. To the biomedical reaction, the definition of reaction process would be taken into consideration carefully. In my opinion, it would be better to be measured by “step”—namely, the inversion of the genetic counter—than the amount of the outcome of the product. It would be more accurate and controllable.
